# Photosynthesis: The Energy Conversion Process

## Overview
Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants, algae, and some bacteria convert light energy into chemical energy stored in glucose.
**Equation**: 
$$ 6CO_2 + 6H_2O + light \rightarrow C_6H_{12}O_6 + 6O_2 $$

## Stages

### 1. Light-Dependent Reactions
- **Location**: Thylakoid membranes of chloroplasts.
- **Inputs**: Light energy, H2O.
- **Outputs**: ATP, NADPH, O2 (byproduct).
- **Process**: Sunlight excites electrons in chlorophyll. Water is split (photolysis) to release reactions.

### 2. Calvin Cycle (Light-Independent)
- **Location**: Stroma of chloroplasts.
- **Inputs**: ATP, NADPH, CO2.
- **Outputs**: Glucose (G3P).
- **Process**: Carbon fixation converts CO2 into organic molecules.

## Importance
- Produces oxygen for aerobic life.
- Base of the food web (primary producers).
- Regulates atmospheric CO2 levels.
